ABOUT

In the depths of the digital realm, a humble frog named Pepe emerged as an unexpected harbinger of internet culture. Born from the imagination of artist Matt Furie, Pepe the Frog leaped onto the screen in the early 2000s through the pages of the webcomic "Boys Club." With his wide grin and expressive eyes, Pepe's whimsical appearance made him an icon in the making.

As Pepe hopped across the internet, he found himself in a myriad of strange and humorous scenarios. His simple design and relatable expressions made him a chameleon of emotion, capable of conveying joy, sarcasm, sadness, and frustration with a simple image and a few words. He became the digital embodiment of the human experience, a messenger of mirth in the digital wilderness.

Yet, like all legends, Pepe's tale took a twist. Dark clouds gathered on the horizon as he unwittingly ventured into treacherous waters. Some corners of the internet sought to corrupt the innocent frog, transforming him into a symbol of hate, bigotry, and extremism. A shadow fell upon Pepe, casting doubt upon his once-pristine reputation.
